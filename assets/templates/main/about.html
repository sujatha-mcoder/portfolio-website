Over the last few years, WSNs have gained popularity across industries and within the research community. WSN is a network made up of numerous sensor devices that spread across over the large area to captured environmental conditions. WSN nodes communicate with each other and transmit the information without wire to central device which act a base station (BS) for storage and analysis. These sensor networks operate in remote and challenging environments, and face security issues due to their limited energy, power and computing resources. Past research on security challenges in WSNs, categorizes threats, and introduces a generic security model tailored to address these concerns . The nature of WSNs is ad-hoc and has susceptibility to the attacks and security. Security is a major challenge in ad-hoc nature networks due to their constrained resources are battery power, storage, memory and bandwidth, and processing capabilities. Furthermore, as they operate in unsupervised environments, these nodes are vulnerable to attacks. These attacks deplete node resources, particularly power, and disrupt their functions, making them some of the most common threats to WSNs are DoS attack, node compromising attack, Sybil attack, jamming attack, eavesdropping attack, etc.. Investigating these attacks is critical for improving the security, reliability, and efficiency of the WSN. To protect the integrity of the surveillance systems that maintain continuous operations in real-world applications such as smart grid systems , healthcare , financial trading etc. It is essential that DoS attacks to be identified and countered. It is important to ensure the security of WSN from DoS attacks, an effective intrusion detection system (IDS) is required to indentify various DoS attacks. ML classifier is used to detect attack WSN-DS dataset . ML based techniques usually suffer with overfitting or under fitting problem, moreover, it is a trained model not programmed. In our study, we proposed an integrated approach of rule-based and ML based technique. In WSNs, prevention based techniques such as cryptography are resource-intensive and less suitable. Detection based methods including misuse/signature detection and anomaly detection are more appropriate. Misuse detection identifies known attack patterns but not new ones.
